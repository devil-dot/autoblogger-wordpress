It's important to distinguish between probiotics and prebiotics. While both are important for gut health, they are different substances with different functions. Probiotics are live microorganisms that offer health benefits when consumed in adequate amounts. They can be found in foods like yogurt, kefir, and sauerkraut, as well as in dietary supplements. On the other hand, prebiotics are a type of fiber that is not digestible by the human body but can be fermented by bacteria in the gut. Prebiotic fibers can be found in foods like onions, garlic, bananas, and asparagus, as well as in dietary supplements.
